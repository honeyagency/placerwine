WordPress Overview

If you're somewhat familiar with WordPress, feel free to skip to the next section, but it's not integral to understanding your new website. WordPress is a Content Management System (CMS) - at a basic level it performs the following functions:

  1. Displaying content to users. (Frontend)
  2. Providing a secure, simple location for site-owners to update that content. (Backend)

At Honey Agency, we custom-build the backend so site-owners don't need to worry about code or formatting, there's a few fields that you fill out and we take care of formatting via code. We usually re-size images for you as well; so unless you see a note about image sizes when you are uploading them we're taking care of the size on the front-end.

WordPress breaks down content into pages and posts. A page is the most general form of webpage content: you have some text, possibly an image, and you can access that content via a URL. A post is similar to a page, but it's hierarchy is based on the model of a blog: you generally have the text and image content that you'd have with a page, but posts are displayed in series and usually have a date and an author. These are generalizations, but are the basic grounds of how a lot of the web is built.

At Honey Agency, we try to separate content-types into custom versions of a post to make the website hierarchy more simple and streamlined for clients. For example at honeyagency.com, we have a team page with individual team members so we have a custom post that's called Team Member. When you want to add a new team member you don't have to custom-format anything, you just click Add New Team Member on the backend; once you fill out all the content required the team member is automatically populated in different places on the site. Depending on your site, you might have a few custom-posts that we'll go over in the sections below.

Logging In

Logging in to your WordPress site is easy: just add /wp-login to the end of your URL, use the Password and Username (or email) provided and you're on your way. Once you login you will see what's called the WordPress dashboard-from here you can get anywhere you need to update content. If you look at the homepage of your site while logged in, there will be a black-bar along the top of the page with links to various site functions.

Media Library

One of the perks of having a site with a CMS is that you have a centralized location for any images you've added site-wide. So anytime you upload images, PDFs, audio, etc to a post, a page, or a custom post they'll be added to a folder similar to the Photos or Pictures folder on your computer.

To get to your Media Library, click the Media Library navigation item in the left-hand side menu in the WordPress dashboard. You'll be presented with a gallery of all the images currently on your site.

Adding a Media Item

To add a media item, click Add New from the Media Library. From here you can drag-and-drop however many images you like (note the Maximum upload file size, this is per-file) and they'll automatically be added and have URL's generated for them. Usually, you'll simply upload images or PDFs directly to a post or page, in which case they'll be added to this library.

Adding/Editing a Page

In the WordPress overview, you heard that the page is the most basic form of content in WordPress, this is how we'll add & edit those.

Adding A New Page

Once you're logged in you can add a new page from either:

  1. The black bar along the top of the site, hover (tap on mobile) over + New and click Page
  2. From the dashboard, there's a navigation item on the left-hand side of the page called Pages. Click this and click Add New.

You'll be taken to the backend of a page with no title or content, when you add a title the URL will automatically be generated for you. If you want to change that URL click the area of text you want to change and edit the text, WordPress will automatically convert your text to lower-case and will replace spaces with hyphens to be URL-compatible.

Content

After you have your title and URL set, you can use the area below to add content. The large white text-box is called a WYSIWYG (What You See Is What You Get) and will operate similarly to Microsoft Word, Apple Pages, or Google Docs.

Published / Draft / Schedulded / Private

You'll see a bar on the right-hand side of the page with options for publishing the page. To publish (make the page go live) the page, click Publish. If you don't want people to see the content, you can choose Save Draft. If you want to schedule the page, you can change the date where you see Publish immediately. If you want to publish the page but only want people who are logged into the site to see it (other admins) you can choose Private under the Visibility option.

Editing a Page

To edit a page that is already created, you can either click Edit Page in the black top-bar when you're looking at the page from the front-end, or you can find the page by clicking Pages in the dashboard and clicking the blue title of the page you're looking for from the list of pages.

Adding Featured Images

Featured images are used as the header image and the social media "share image" (the little preview-picture people see when you share a link on a social media platform) if there isn't a custom header image on your site. These are located on the right-hand side of the page when you're editing a page, a post, or any type of custom post. Click Set Featured Image and you'll see a pop-up that should be populated with your media library. You can select a previously uploaded image or click the Upload Files tab at the top of this pop-up to upload an image.

Updating Site Options

Site options is the location where you’ll find places to update the following:

  • Social Media URLs
  • Defaults
  • Search Box Content
Social Media URLs

Social media URLs are simple to update, and when the links are left blank it will hide the icons on the front-end, making it easy to toggle on and off.

To add a link, click the pencil icon, a box with a place to add the link as well as a title will pop up, all you need to do is fill those in.

Defaults

Default Site Image

This is the image that's displayed for social media, link sharing, etc when the shared page has no image, it should be large, but you most likely won’t need to update it.

Map Center

This is the default center of any map on the website, it allows you to enter an address and is powered by google maps.

404 Text

This is where you can edit the copy that is displayed on the 404 page.

Search Box Content

Search Modal Title

This is the title in the search pop-up

Search modal text

This is the explanation text in the search pop-up

Search Form Placeholder

This is the content that is grayed-out within the search field.

Search Form Button

This is the text in the search button.

Search link text

This is the text that explains the search text

Search Links

Add a few links that might more quickly get people to what they're looking for while searching.

Adding Content to a Winery

Winery content is important because each winery influences the map and the content on the Explore Our Wineries page. The wineries are broken up into 5 sections. The featured image you learned about earlier in the documentation is used for the header image as well as the image that displays next to the winery introduction on the Explore Our Wineries page.

The five sections wineries are broken into are:

  • Content
  • Amenities & Varietals
  • Press & Awards
  • Days & Hours
  • Contact Information
Content

This section contains the following:

Logo

This displays in the sidebar, it’s automatically resized, so use an image 500px or larger.

Short Description

This is a short one-sentence description that displays on the list of wineries.

Winery Content

This is the main content that displays on the left-hand-side of the winery pages.

Events Page Link

If the winery has an events page, this will link to it directly. This is optional and is separate from the winery website URL.

Amenities & Varietals

Amenities and Varietals are shared between wineries. That means there are a finite number of amenities and varietals to choose from, more can be added to each but a smaller number will be more beneficial to the user.

Amenities

Each Amenity item has an optional text area on the right that can be used to provide more information about a specific amenity. For example “Please call ahead for busses or limos”

Varietals Produced

Start typing the name of a varietal and it will appear, click it and you’re done! This is used to filter varietals on the Explore Our Wineries page. If a winery wants to go into more detail on their varietals, they can do so in the content area.

Press & Awards

Each of these sections have repeating content areas, so you “add an item” and it will appear below the previous one.

Press

  • Image - (a 120px x 120px image can be added)
  • Publication - Name of publication
  • Press Title - Title of the press item
  • Press Link - Link to the press item

Awards

  • Image - (a 120px x 120px image can be added)
  • Award Subject - The competition or subject in question
  • Award Title - The award title
  • Award Link - An optional link to the award
Days & Hours

Days Open (For winery filtering)

This data informs filtering on the winery page, check each box that the winery is open.

Open Dates

This displays on the winery page in the sidebar, it’s useful for adding date information that’s specific to the day. For example (Tuesdays, please make appointment)

Open Hours

This is similar to the Open Dates setting, add the time open, for example a line might read: 11am to 5pm and another line could read Open until 8pm on Fridays

Contact Information

Address

This is a google map powered search box that will add a pin automatically to the Explore Our Wineries page and will add a directions link to the winery sidebar.

Phone

This displays in the sidebar on the winery page.

Website

Here you can add a link to the winery page, the text will automatically be filled.

Adding content to events

Events have a few optional boxes to display information on the event page.

Event Date Start
Required, this displays on the homepage, sets the order of events, and displays on the event itself.

Event Date End

Not required, but useful if an event happens over multiple days, such as a weekend.

Cost

If the event has a cost, enter here.

Location

The location of an event, text instead of a map is used so multiple locations or fuzzy information may be added.

Hours

Text area to add information about the hours of the event.

Tickets Button

If the event requires tickets, you may provide a link to the ticketing service here.

Event Introduction

Short piece of content, this will be displayed on the homepage.

Event Content

This is a standard WYSIWYG to add content and images on the event page.

Event Details

Each of these sections is a good way to add structured content to an event. It has a title and a content area. It would be useful, say, to give a list of which wineries are involved in the event.
